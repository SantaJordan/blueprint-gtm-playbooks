Blueprint GTM Playbook for Mixmax

Focus: Mid-Funnel B2B Sales & Consulting | Generated: December 2024

Who the Hell is Jordan Crawford?

Founder of Blueprint GTM. Built a business by scraping 25M+ job posts to find company pain points. Believes the Predictable Revenue model is dead. Thinks mounting an AI SDR on outdated methodology is like putting a legless robot on a horse—no one gets anywhere, and it still shits along the way.

The core philosophy is simple: The message isn't the problem. The LIST is the message. When you know exactly who to target and why they need you right now, the message writes itself.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical Mixmax SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question about Your Sales Process Hi [First Name], I noticed on LinkedIn that your company recently expanded its sales team. Congrats on the growth! I wanted to reach out because we work with companies like Gong, Canva, and Meltwater to help their AEs close more deals with less effort. Mixmax helps teams: • Track email engagement in real-time • Automate follow-up sequences • Book meetings with one click Would you have 15 minutes next week to explore how we might be able to help [Company Name] hit quota faster? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: The prospect is an expert. They've seen this template 1,000 times. There's zero indication you actually understand their specific situation. "I noticed you're growing" is visible to every vendor on the planet. Delete.

The New Way: Intelligence-Driven GTM

Blueprint GTM flips the entire approach. Instead of interrupting prospects with pitches, you deliver insights so valuable they'd pay consulting fees to receive them. You become the person who helps them see around corners, not another vendor in their inbox.

This requires two fundamental shifts:

1. Hard Data Over Soft Signals

Stop: "I see you're hiring sales people" (job postings - everyone sees this)

Start: "Your 7 RFP deadlines between now and March 15 - SAM.gov solicitations #W912QR-25-R-0047, #FA8770-25-R-0012..." (government database with record numbers)

2. Mirror Situations, Don't Pitch Solutions

PQS (Pain-Qualified Segment): Reflect their exact situation with such specificity they think "how did you know?" Use verifiable data - tech stack detection, government records, funding data with dates.

PVP (Permissionless Value Proposition): Deliver immediate value they can use today - contracting officer patterns, competitor tech upgrades, benchmark analysis - whether they buy or not.

Mixmax PQS Plays: Mirroring Exact Situations

These messages demonstrate such precise understanding of the prospect's current situation that they feel genuinely seen. Every claim traces to verifiable data sources.

PQS 9.3/10 Federal Consulting

Play 1: RFP Deadline Cluster Detection

What's the play? Identify federal consulting firms with 5+ RFP deadlines clustered in a 21-day window. During these "proposal factory" periods, firms submit and move on - zero systematic follow-up with contracting officers. Their BD partners are too buried in deadlines to nurture pending proposals.

Why this works: You're counting their EXACT deadlines from government data. They know they're busy - they DON'T know you can see exactly HOW many proposals are competing for their attention. The "automated sequences" offer solves a problem they're actively experiencing but haven't solved.

Data source: SAM.gov Opportunities API - NAICS 541611/541613 filter, response_deadline field. FREE access with API key. Real-time updates.

The message:

Subject: Your 7 March RFP deadlines I counted 7 active solicitations in your NAICS codes with deadlines between now and March 15 - Army Corps, GSA OASIS, and 5 others. That's proposal factory mode. During deadline clusters like this, most firms submit and move on. No follow-up with contracting officers, no engagement tracking, no re-engagement when proposals get reopened during evaluation. Our federal consulting clients set up automated 4-touch sequences before deadline season. They fire automatically after submission - partners don't touch them. Reply "sequences" and I'll show you the exact templates they use.
PQS 8.6/10 B2B SaaS

Play 2: Tech Stack Gap Detection

What's the play? Identify post-Series A/B SaaS companies using Salesforce or HubSpot but without a sales engagement layer. Their AEs are manually logging activities, switching between tabs, and have zero visibility into which prospects are engaging with their proposals.

Why this works: You're telling them something specific about THEIR tech stack that they know is true but haven't quantified. The "48 hours = extra AE" framing makes the invisible cost tangible. The "5 minutes, no CRM changes" removes implementation fear.

Data source: BuiltWith API ($295/mo) for tech detection + LinkedIn/Crunchbase for AE count. Verifiable - they can check their own domain on BuiltWith.

The message:

Subject: Your 8 AEs without engagement tracking I ran your domain through tech detection. You have Salesforce, 8 AEs (LinkedIn), and no sales engagement layer. That means your team is manually logging every email, manually scheduling every follow-up, and has zero visibility into who's actually engaging with proposals. Quick math: 8 AEs × 6 hours/week on CRM admin = 48 hours of selling time lost weekly. That's an extra AE's worth of capacity you're paying for but not using. I can show you what it looks like when your AEs see every open, click, and reply in real-time inside Gmail. 5 minutes, no CRM changes, works tomorrow. Worth a look?

Mixmax PVP Plays: Delivering Immediate Value

These messages provide actionable intelligence before asking for anything. The prospect can use this value today whether they respond or not. That's the power of permissionless value.

PVP 9.6/10 Federal Consulting

Play 1: Contracting Officer Intelligence

What's the play? For each active pursuit, pull the contracting officer's historical patterns from USASpending - average days to award, communication preferences, incumbent win rates, small business set-aside percentages. Deliver this intelligence as immediate value they can use to tailor their follow-up approach.

Why this works: This is GOLD they'd pay a consultant for. You're showing them patterns in public data they've never analyzed. "67 days vs 52 avg" and "email preference" are immediately actionable. They can verify every claim in USASpending.gov themselves.

Data source: SAM.gov (CO name, solicitation) + USASpending API (historical awards, award dates, awardee names). FREE. 100% verifiable government data.

The message:

Subject: Your Army Corps CO's pattern You're pursuing solicitation #W912QR-25-R-0047. The contracting officer, James Mitchell, has awarded 12 contracts in the last 3 years. His patterns: awards 67 days post-deadline (vs. 52 avg), prefers email follow-up over phone, awards to small business set-asides 58% of the time. Most firms treat all COs the same. Mitchell's longer timeline means you have an extra 2 weeks of follow-up opportunity most competitors miss. His email preference means phone calls might actually hurt you. I can pull similar intel on your other active COs. Want the full breakdown?
PVP 8.6/10 B2B SaaS

Play 2: Stuck G2 Researcher

What's the play? Identify companies that have been actively researching sales engagement tools on G2 for 45+ days but haven't purchased. They've compared Outreach, Salesloft, and others. They're stuck - likely on price or implementation complexity. Address their specific objection directly.

Why this works: You're calling out exactly what they've been doing (they were on G2, they know it). The "budget or complexity" framing shows you understand the REAL blockers, not surface objections. The direct question "What's holding up your decision?" cuts through and demands an honest answer.

Data source: G2 Buyer Intent API (category visits, comparison signals) + BuiltWith (verify no tool purchased). Requires G2 subscription (~$500-2000/mo).

The message:

Subject: Your Outreach vs. Salesloft research I noticed your team has been researching sales engagement tools on G2 for about 6 weeks - you've compared Outreach, Salesloft, and a few others. You still haven't implemented anything (I checked your tech stack). Most teams get stuck for one of two reasons: (1) enterprise pricing feels too expensive for your team size, or (2) worried about implementation complexity. If either of those resonates - we're the alternative companies your size choose after hitting those walls. What's actually holding up your decision? Happy to address it directly rather than give you another generic demo.
PVP 9.1/10 Federal Consulting

Bonus Play: Contract Cliff Calculator

What's the play? Calculate the firm's "contract cliff" - percentage of active contract revenue ending in the next 90 days. Show them the math: if 65% of revenue expires by March 31, here's how many proposals they need to win at a 20% win rate to bridge the gap.

Why this works: You've done analysis on THEIR business using public data. The "revenue cliff" concept creates urgency they may not have quantified. The bottleneck insight ("not proposal quality, it's follow-up bandwidth") reframes the problem in a solvable way.

Data source: USASpending API (contractor_name, period_of_performance_end, dollars_obligated). FREE. 100% verifiable.

The message:

Subject: Your March contract cliff I pulled your USASpending data. You have $2.1M in contracts ending by March 31 - that's roughly 65% of your active federal revenue. To bridge that gap at a 20% win rate, you need 25-30 active proposals. The bottleneck isn't proposal quality - it's follow-up bandwidth. Your partners are too busy delivering on current contracts to systematically follow up with contracting officers during evaluation periods. I can show you how other boutique consulting firms use automated sequences to maintain 5-touch follow-up on every proposal without adding headcount. Reply if you want the math on your specific situation.

The Transformation

Notice the difference? Traditional outreach talks about YOUR product and YOUR benefits. Blueprint GTM talks about THEIR situation and THEIR challenges using verifiable data they can look up themselves.

The shift is simple but profound:

Stop sending messages about what you do. Start sending intelligence about what they need to know right now. When you lead with "7 March RFP deadlines" or "8 AEs without engagement tracking" instead of "I see you're growing," you're not another sales email - you're the person who actually did the research.

For Mixmax specifically: The mid-funnel sales use case is about helping prospects SEE the invisible cost of manual follow-up. Federal consultants don't realize they're losing RFPs due to follow-up gaps. B2B SaaS VPs don't realize their AEs are spending 6 hours/week on admin that could be automated. Your job is to make that invisible cost visible - with specific numbers they can verify.

Implementation Priority:

Start with Federal Consulting (FREE data): SAM.gov and USASpending APIs are free. The three federal consulting plays (RFP Deadline Cluster, CO Intel, Contract Cliff) all scored 9.0+ and require zero data investment.

Then add B2B SaaS (paid data): BuiltWith ($295/mo) + G2 Intent (~$500-2000/mo) unlock the tech stack gap and stuck researcher plays. Start with BuiltWith alone for the tech gap play.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify prospects experiencing specific, urgent challenges where Mixmax's mid-funnel engagement solutions provide unique value - and proving you've done the homework with verifiable data.

The companies that master this approach don't compete on features. They compete on intelligence.
