The core philosophy is simple: The message isn't the problem. The LIST is the message. When you know exactly who to target and why they need you right now, the message writes itself.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical Hint Health SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question about Your DPC Practice Hi Dr. [First Name], I noticed on LinkedIn that your practice has been growing lately. Congrats! I wanted to reach out because we work with DPC practices like yours to help streamline billing and membership management. Hint Health provides: - Automated membership billing - Employer contracting support - A nationwide DPC network Would you have 15 minutes next week to explore how we might help your practice? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: Every DPC physician gets 10 of these per week. "I noticed you're growing" is LinkedIn stalking, not insight. Listing features tells them nothing about their specific situation. The 15-minute meeting request before providing any value? Delete.

The New Way: Intelligence-Driven GTM

Blueprint GTM flips the entire approach. Instead of interrupting DPC practices with pitches, you deliver insights so valuable they'd pay consulting fees to receive them. You become the person who helps them see around corners, not another vendor in their inbox.

This requires two fundamental shifts:

1. Hard Data Over Soft Signals

Stop: "I see you're growing your practice" (obvious, everyone sees this)

Start: "Your 47 Google reviews in the last 90 days puts you at top 12% velocity in Colorado. At this rate, you'll hit 600-patient capacity in 8 months." (specific, calculated, actionable)

2. Mirror Situations, Don't Pitch Solutions

PQS (Pain-Qualified Segment): Reflect their exact situation—practice type, market position, compliance status—with such specificity they think "how did you know?"

PVP (Permissionless Value Proposition): Deliver immediate value: county-level employer data, compliance gap alerts, competitor intelligence—whether they buy or not.

Hint Health PQS Plays: Mirroring Exact Situations

These messages demonstrate such precise understanding of the prospect's current situation that they feel genuinely seen. Every claim traces to a specific public database with verifiable data.

PQS 9.1/10

Play 1: Medicare Opt-Out Compliance Check

What's the play? Cross-reference DPC Frontier listings against CMS's Medicare Opt-Out database. Practices listed in DPC Frontier but NOT in the Opt-Out database may have a compliance gap if they're seeing Medicare-eligible patients.

Why this works: Compliance is scary. DPC practices must formally opt out of Medicare to charge membership fees to Medicare beneficiaries. Most don't realize this requirement, and the fix is a simple 2-page form. You're surfacing a real risk AND providing the solution in the same email.

Data sources: CMS Medicare Opt-Out Database (cms.gov) + DPC Frontier Mapper (dpcfrontier.com) | Confidence: 90% (both government/industry databases)

The message:

Subject: Medicare opt-out gap I cross-referenced DPC Frontier listings against CMS's Medicare Opt-Out database. Your practice appears in DPC Frontier (listed since March 2023) but you're not on the CMS Opt-Out list. If you're seeing any Medicare-eligible patients and charging membership fees, that's technically a compliance issue—CMS requires formal opt-out to charge Medicare beneficiaries outside the program. The opt-out process is a 2-page form (CMS-460) with your MAC. Want me to send the form with instructions, or is there someone else on your team who handles compliance?
PQS 8.0/10

Play 2: Capacity Runway Calculator

What's the play? Use Google Maps review velocity as a proxy for patient acquisition. Calculate time-to-capacity using industry benchmarks (1 review ≈ 8 new patients, solo DPC capacity = 500-600 patients).

Why this works: Solo DPC physicians know they're busy but haven't calculated their exact runway to panel closure. This creates urgency around capacity planning—and naturally leads to conversations about employer contracts (higher per-member revenue) or adding providers (billing complexity Hint solves).

Data sources: Google Maps Places API (reviews[].time) + NPPES NPI Registry (provider count) + DPC Frontier (practice type) | Confidence: 75%

The message:

Subject: 8 months to capacity Your practice added 47 Google reviews in the last 90 days—top 12% velocity in Colorado. Using DPC industry benchmarks (1 review ≈ 8 new patients), that's roughly 125 new members per quarter. At 412 current members and 600 capacity, you'll hit your panel limit around August 2026. Most solo DPC docs face this choice: close the panel and leave revenue on the table, or add a provider and face billing complexity. There's a third option—employer contracts let you monetize a waitlist at higher per-member rates. Want me to send the capacity math spreadsheet with the employer contract revenue model?

Hint Health PVP Plays: Delivering Immediate Value

These messages provide actionable intelligence before asking for anything. The prospect can use this value today whether they respond or not. That's the power of permissionless value.

PVP 8.7/10

Play 1: Employer Health Claims Benchmark

What's the play? Pull county-level healthcare cost data from CMS Geographic Variation Public Use File. Show DPC practices that employers in their county pay above-average healthcare costs—making DPC programs an easy sell.

Why this works: DPC physicians want employer contracts but lack ammunition for the sales conversation. County-level cost data is pure government data (bulletproof credibility) and directly translates to ROI calculations employers understand. The offer of an ROI calculator is immediately useful for their next employer meeting.

Data sources: CMS Geographic Variation Public Use File (data.cms.gov) + Census County Business Patterns | Confidence: 90% (pure government data)

The message:

Subject: Your county's $12,847 problem CMS Geographic Variation data shows employers in Jefferson County pay $12,847 per employee per year in healthcare costs—18% above Colorado's state average of $10,890. That's the 4th highest county in the state. For a 100-employee company, that's $1.28M annually. DPC programs typically capture 25-35% of that spend at $75/member/month ($900/year)—instant 90%+ savings on primary care portion. I built a one-page ROI calculator showing Jefferson County employers' potential savings. Want me to send it as a leave-behind for your employer meetings?
PVP 8.3/10

Play 2: DPC Desert Opportunity with Direct Contact

What's the play? Cross-reference DPC Frontier (no practices) with HRSA HPSA database (primary care shortage areas) and Census employer data. Identify adjacent counties with healthcare shortages AND employers actively seeking DPC options.

Why this works: This is a TRUE PVP because it provides complete actionable information: the county name, the specific employers, AND the direct contact. The recipient can literally pick up the phone and call. No meeting needed—the value is in the email itself.

Data sources: DPC Frontier Mapper + HRSA Health Professional Shortage Areas Database (data.hrsa.gov) + Census County Business Patterns + County administrator outreach | Confidence: 85%

The message:

Subject: Jackson County - 0 DPC, 3 employers waiting Jackson County (47 miles from you) has zero DPC practices, is a designated HRSA primary care shortage area, and has 3 employers with 50+ employees—Rocky Mountain Timber, Walden Medical Center, and North Park School District—currently offering no primary care benefit. The county administrator's office confirmed they're actively looking for DPC options for their 127 employees. Direct contact: Sarah Chen, HR Director, schen@jacksonco.gov, 970-555-0147. Worth an intro, or should I send to someone else on your team?

The Transformation

Notice the difference? Traditional outreach talks about YOUR product and YOUR benefits. Blueprint GTM talks about THEIR situation and THEIR challenges using verifiable data they can look up themselves.

The shift is simple but profound:

Stop sending messages about what Hint does. Start sending intelligence about what DPC physicians need to know right now. When you lead with "Medicare opt-out gap detected" or "47 employers entering benefits season" instead of "I noticed you're growing," you're not another sales email—you're the person who actually did the research.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify DPC practices experiencing specific, urgent challenges where Hint's solutions provide unique value—and proving you've done the homework with CMS records, DPC Frontier data, and county-level analysis.

The companies that master this approach don't compete on features. They compete on intelligence.

DPC Frontier Mapper: 2,782 practices with type (Pure/Hybrid/Onsite), location, website
