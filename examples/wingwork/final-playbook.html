Lessons Learned: When Blueprint GTM Works vs. Doesn't

✅ Blueprint GTM Works Best For:

  • Regulated industries with public compliance violations: EPA (environmental), OSHA (safety), CMS (healthcare quality), FDA (food/pharma), state licensing boards
  • Observable trigger events: New permits issued, inspection deadlines, quality rating changes, expansion permits filed
  • Non-obvious synthesis available: Can calculate something prospect doesn't know (days to deadline, percentile ranking, violation clustering)

⚠️ Blueprint GTM Struggles With:

  • Internal operational pain: Poor software UX, manual processes, legacy system frustrations (no public record)
  • Private business problems: Revenue challenges, profitability issues, team dysfunction (not externally observable)
  • Industries without heavy regulation: SaaS, professional services, retail (unless they create environmental/safety violations)

The WingWork Category:

WingWork falls into a HYBRID zone:

  • ✅ Operates in regulated industry (aviation)
  • ✅ Can identify WHO operates WHAT aircraft (FAA databases)
  • ❌ Pain points are operational, not compliance violations
  • ❌ No public record of poor maintenance tracking systems
  • ⚠️ Can connect industry crises (mechanic shortage) to operator circumstances (mixed fleets) for 7.8-8.0/10 messaging

The Honest Truth: Not every B2B SaaS company should use Blueprint GTM as their PRIMARY strategy. For operational pain points without public data trails, product-led growth + inbound content + industry partnerships often deliver better ROI than attempting to force intelligence-driven outreach.

Use Blueprint GTM where it's STRONG (compliance violation industries), and use complementary approaches where it's WEAK (operational efficiency tools).
