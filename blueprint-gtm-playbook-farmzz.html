The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what typical ag-tech SDRs are doing right now. They're buying lists from farming directories, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical AgTech SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question about Your Farm Hi Jean-Pierre, I noticed your farm is listed on Bienvenue à la Ferme. Congrats on the great reviews! I wanted to reach out because we work with farms like Ferme de la Vallée and Domaine des Chênes to help with customer engagement. Our platform offers SMS marketing, email campaigns, and loyalty programs. We've helped farms increase repeat visits by 20-30%. Would you have 15 minutes next week to explore how we might help La Chaumière? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: The farmer is an expert at being interrupted. They've seen this template 1,000 times from software vendors. There's zero indication you actually understand THEIR specific situation - their certification, their products, their commune's demographics. It's interruption disguised as personalization. Delete.

The shift for French agricultural markets:

Stop sending messages about what Farmzz does. Start sending intelligence about what the farmer needs to know right now. Key Insight for the French Market:
French agricultural producers (producteurs agricoles) doing direct sales (vente directe/circuits courts) represent a market of ~90,000 farms. They are sophisticated operators who value concrete, specific data over generic promises. Messages in French with local cultural context significantly outperform English templates. Government data sources (INSEE, Agence BIO, data.gouv.fr) carry high credibility.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify farmers experiencing specific, urgent challenges where Farmzz's SMS/email loyalty platform provides unique value - and proving you've done the homework with verifiable French government data.
